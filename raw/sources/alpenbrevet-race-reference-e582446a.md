## Race Overview

The Alpenbrevet is an annual alpine gran fondo based in Andermatt, Switzerland. Athletes ride one of several route options crossing multiple high alpine passes in a single day.

**Platinum route (the hardest, used by this athlete):**
- Distance: ~210–215 km (varies slightly by GPS and exact edition)
- Elevation: 4,700–5,100m depending on direction and GPS accuracy
- Category: ultra-endurance (10–15 hours depending on level)
- Start: Andermatt (~1,440m altitude), early morning (~5:00–6:00 am local time)
- Organised event with feed stations but largely self-supported pacing

---

## The Four Passes

| Pass | Altitude | Key characteristic |
|---|---|---|
| Furka | 2,429m | Steep west face, gentler east (Realp) side |
| Nufenen (Passo della Novena) | 2,478m | Highest paved pass in Switzerland. Steep from Ulrichen (north); gentler from Airolo (south) |
| Lukmanier (Passo del Lucomagno) | 1,914m | Lowest of the four. Gradual on both sides. Longest climb from Disentis side |
| Oberalp | 2,044m | Relatively gentle on both sides. Often the "recovery" pass |

## Route Directions

The Platinum route alternates direction between editions.

### 2022 direction — West to East
**Order:** Furka → Nufenen → Lukmanier → Oberalp

- Furka and Nufenen (two hardest passes) ridden **early while fresh**
- Oberalp last — the easiest pass, a good "finishing" climb
- Athletes hit the hardest terrain with the most glycogen and least fatigue

### 2024 direction — East to West (reversed)
**Order:** Oberalp → Lukmanier → Nufenen → Furka

- Oberalp first — gentle, warm-up pass
- Lukmanier second — moderate
- **Nufenen and Furka in the final third of the race, after 130–160km**
- Athletes hit the two hardest climbs deep in fatigue, with glycogen depleted
- Significantly more demanding to execute well

**Key insight for pacing:** the reversed direction front-loads easy terrain and saves the hardest passes for when fatigue is highest. Nutrition strategy must be more aggressive, and pacing on the early passes must be more conservative than feels natural.

## Practical Conditions

- **Start time:** ~5:00–6:00 am. First 2 hours are cold (8–12°C). Gilet + arm warmers recommended for start.
- **Temperature range:** typically 8–20°C throughout the day. High passes can be colder and windy.
- **Feed stations:** organised stops every ~40–60km. Pre-plan which stops to use vs. skip.
- **Surface:** all paved. No technical descending required, but speed can be high (>80 km/h on some descents).
- **Traffic:** road is partially closed or managed in early hours; increases as day progresses.
