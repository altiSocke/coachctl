# Dillier Classic — Race Reference

> Personal results, race strategy, power card, and cramp prevention notes live in the events table (`propose_race_*` / `apply_race_*`).

---

## Race Overview

The Dillier Classic is an annual road cycling sportive in central Switzerland, named after Silvan Dillier.

- **Distance:** ~127 km
- **Elevation:** ~2,600m
- **Format:** 8 timed climb segments. The athlete's final result is the aggregate time across all 8 timed climbs only. Flat and descent sections are not timed.
- **Location:** Surental / Luzern region, Switzerland
- **Difficulty:** demanding for amateurs — significant climbing compressed into 5–6 hours

---

## Course Structure

The race has 8 designated timed climbs. The group dynamic changes significantly across the race:

**Early climbs (1–4, approximately km 15–55):** after each timed climb, the group regroups at the summit or designated collection point. Athletes who are dropped on early climbs will be retrieved. This encourages conservative early pacing.

**Later climbs (5–8, approximately km 55–112):** the group NO LONGER waits after timed climbs. Athletes dropped from the group must ride solo for the remaining flat/descent sections between climbs. Riding solo at 35–40 km/h costs ~25–30% more power than drafting in the group.

**Critical implication:** staying in the group past km 80–90 is the single highest-value tactical decision. An athlete who overcooked early climbs and gets dropped at km 88 will lose 5–10 minutes on flat sections alone, in addition to suffering through the final climbs alone.

## Tactical Notes (course-specific, any athlete)

### The flat section km 67–88 — cramp risk zone

After climb 6 and before climb 7, there is a 20+ km flat/rolling section where the group rides at high speed (35–40 km/h):
- Legs are already fatigued from 6 timed climbs
- High-speed flat riding encourages a lower cadence and harder gearing
- Athletes who reduce fuelling or sodium intake here pay the price on climb 7

**General protocol:** spin ≥90 rpm on every flat section. Maintain fuelling and sodium intake continuously. Stretch quads on descents before this section.
