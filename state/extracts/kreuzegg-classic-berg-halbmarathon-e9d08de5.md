### Key segments

| Segment | Distance | Altitude | Character |
|---|---|---|---|
| Start → Bütschwil | 0–5 km | ~620 m flat | Flat/rolling, mostly asphalt |
| Bütschwil → Dietfurt | 5–10 km | 620→600 m | Slight descent, flat — 10km finish / DUO relay handoff |
| Dietfurt → Kengelbach | 10–13 km | 600→700 m | Gradual climb begins |
| Kengelbach → Krinau | 13–16 km | 700→850 m | Sustained climb, ~50 m/km |
| Krinau → Älplispitz | 16–21 km | 850→1230 m | **Crux: steep mountain climb, ~76 m/km avg** |
| Älplispitz → Ziel | 21–21.9 km | 1230→1100 m | Short descent to finish |

**Key insight:** The first 10 km are essentially flat (net neutral). All the climbing is concentrated in the second half, with the hardest 5 km being km 16–21 (+380 m). This is a classic "save your legs" race — go out easy, attack the climb.

## Race character & coaching notes

- **Type:** Mountain half marathon, point-to-point, net uphill
- **Difficulty:** Moderate-hard. The flat first half is deceptive — conserve energy for the big climb km 16–21
- **Pacing strategy:** Run the first 10 km at easy/conversational effort (Z2). Begin controlled effort at Dietfurt. Save a surge for Krinau → Älplispitz. The final descent to the finish is short (~1 km, -130 m)
- **Terrain:** Mix of road and trail. Trail shoes or road shoes with grip recommended
- **Weather:** Late May in Toggenburg — can be cold/wet at altitude (~1200 m). Carry a light layer
- **Historical note:** Previously hosted Swiss Mountain Running Championships (2018). Established race in Ostschweiz
- **Results history:** 2021, 2019, 2018 results available at raceresult.com

## Grade-adjusted pace estimate

For a runner with flat HM pace ~5:30/km (rFTP ~5:00/km):
- Flat section (0–10 km): target ~5:30–5:45/km
- Climbing section (10–16 km, ~50 m/km): GAP adds ~45 s/km → effective effort ~6:15/km
- Crux (16–21 km, ~76 m/km): GAP adds ~70 s/km → effective effort ~6:40/km
- Expected finish time for recreational runner: **~2:00–2:20h**
- Cut-off pace to Krinau (km 16 in 2:15h): ~8:26/km — very generous
