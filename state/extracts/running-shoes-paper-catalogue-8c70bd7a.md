## Paper 1 — Sun et al. 2020 ⭐ PRIMARY BIOMECHANICS REVIEW

**Title:** Systematic Review of the Role of Footwear Constructions in Running Biomechanics: Implications for Running-Related Injury and Performance
**Authors:** Sun X, Lam WK, Zhang X, Wang J, Fu W
**Journal:** Journal of Sports Science and Medicine
**Year:** 2020 (Feb 24); eCollection 2020 Mar
**PMID:** 32132824
**PMCID:** PMC7039038
**Access:** Free PMC full text
**Type:** Systematic Review (63 studies, databases through Sep 2018)

**Key findings — shoe constructions:**
- **Stiffness:** Increasing midsole bending stiffness at an optimal range improves performance-related variables (energy return); too little = poor energy return, too much = restricted foot motion
- **Cushioning (midsole softness):** Softer midsoles reduce impact forces and loading rates; thicker midsoles provide better shock attenuation but may decrease plantar sensory feedback
- **Minimalist shoes:** Improve running economy and increase Achilles tendon cross-sectional area and stiffness; but increase metatarsophalangeal and ankle joint loading compared to conventional shoes
- **Heel-toe drop:** Evidence insufficient to draw clear conclusions on injury risk; further research needed
- **Heel cup, upper, shoe lace, heel flare, MBT (rocker sole):** Insufficient evidence for guidelines

**Practical summary:**
- No single shoe construction eliminates injury risk across all runners
- Stiffer midsole (e.g., carbon plate) → better RE, longer contact time, greater stride length
- Softer midsole → lower impact forces, but increased energy cost

## Paper 2 — Relph et al. 2022 ⭐ COCHRANE SYSTEMATIC REVIEW

**Title:** Running shoes for preventing lower limb running injuries in adults
**Authors:** Relph N, Greaves H, Armstrong R, Prior TD, Spencer S, Griffiths IB, Dey P, Langley B
**Journal:** Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews
**Year:** 2022 (Aug 22)
**PMID:** 35993829
**PMCID:** PMC9394464
**DOI:** 10.1002/14651858.CD013368.pub2
**Access:** Free PMC full text
**Type:** Cochrane Systematic Review (12 RCTs, n=11,240)

**Key findings:**
- **Neutral/cushioned vs. minimalist:** Little or no difference in injury rates (low-certainty evidence; RR 0.77, 95% CI 0.59–1.01)
- **Soft vs. hard midsole:** Little or no difference in injury rates (low-certainty; RR 0.82, 95% CI 0.61–1.10)
- **Motion control vs. neutral:** Uncertain (very low certainty)
- **Foot-posture-based prescription:** Prescribing shoes based on static foot posture (arch type) does NOT reduce injury rates (moderate-certainty; Rate Ratio 1.03, 95% CI 0.94–1.13) — the most robust finding in the review
- **Overall verdict:** No shoe type convincingly reduces running injury risk in RCTs; evidence quality is low to very low for most comparisons

**Critical implication:** The widely held belief that prescribing motion-control shoes to overpronators, or stability shoes to flat-footed runners, prevents injury is **not supported by the evidence.** Comfort and personal preference are as defensible as biomechanical prescription.

## Paper 3 — Rodrigo-Carranza et al. 2022 ⭐ META-ANALYSIS — LONGITUDINAL BENDING STIFFNESS

**Title:** The effects of footwear midsole longitudinal bending stiffness on running economy and ground contact biomechanics: A systematic review and meta-analysis
**Authors:** Rodrigo-Carranza V, González-Mohíno F, Santos-Concejero J, González-Ravé JM
**Journal:** European Journal of Sport Science
**Year:** 2022 (Oct)
**PMID:** 34369282
**DOI:** 10.1080/17461391.2021.1955014
**Type:** Systematic Review + Meta-Analysis (12 studies)

**Key findings:**
- Increased longitudinal bending stiffness (LBS = stiffer midsole, e.g., carbon plate) improves running economy: **SMD = −0.43** (95% CI −0.58 to −0.28, p<0.001)
- Biomechanical changes: ↑ stride length (+SMD 0.29), ↑ contact time (+SMD 0.17)
- **Curved plate** (like Nike Vaporfly): 3.45% RE improvement vs. no-plate; **flat plate:** no significant improvement — plate geometry matters
- Effect larger at **higher running speeds** — carbon plate shoes more beneficial for faster runners / race conditions
- When shoe mass is matched: 3.15% RE improvement purely from stiffness (confirming plate effect beyond weight saving)

## Paper 4 — Rodrigo-Carranza et al. 2023 — LBS IN TRAINED VS NATIONAL RUNNERS

**Title:** Influence of Running Shoe Longitudinal Bending Stiffness on Running Economy and Performance in Trained and National Level Runners
**Authors:** Rodrigo-Carranza V, Hoogkamer W, Salinero JJ, Rodríguez-Barbero S, González-Ravé JM, González-Mohíno F
**Journal:** Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise
**Year:** 2023 (Dec 1)
**PMID:** 37443458
**DOI:** 10.1249/MSS.0000000000003254
**Type:** Controlled trial (28 male runners, two groups)

**Key findings:**
- Carbon plate shoe improved RE in **trained runners** at both slow (9 km/h, ~4.0%) and fast (13 km/h, ~3.1%) velocities
- In **national-level runners:** improvement only at fast velocity (17 km/h)
- 3000m time trial improved similarly in both groups (~5s faster in carbon plate shoes)
- Biomechanical adaptations differed between groups, suggesting individual kinematic responses

**Implication:** Carbon plate shoes benefit recreational/trained runners across a wide speed range; elite runners may see smaller gains except at race pace.

## Paper 5 — Whiting et al. 2022 — VAPORFLY ON HILLS

**Title:** Metabolic cost of level, uphill, and downhill running in highly cushioned shoes with carbon-fiber plates
**Authors:** Whiting CS, Hoogkamer W, Kram R
**Journal:** Journal of Sport and Health Science
**Year:** 2022 (May); Epub 2021 Nov
**PMID:** 34740871
**PMCID:** PMC9189710
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.jshs.2021.10.004
**Access:** Free PMC
**Type:** Controlled trial (16 competitive male runners)

**Key findings:**
- Nike Vaporfly 4% reduced metabolic power by **3.83% on level**, **2.82% uphill (+3°)**, **2.70% downhill (−3°)** vs. conventional racing shoes
- Uphill benefit significantly smaller than level (p=0.04)
- **Practical:** On hilly race courses (e.g., Alpenbrevet, Dillier Classic), the carbon plate energy savings are ~25–30% lower than on flat courses; still meaningful but not full 4%

## Paper 6 — Tenforde et al. 2023 ⚠️ INJURY RISK — CARBON PLATE

**Title:** Bone Stress Injuries in Runners Using Carbon Fiber Plate Footwear
**Authors:** Tenforde A, Hoenig T, Saxena A, Hollander K
**Journal:** Sports Medicine
**Year:** 2023 (Aug)
**PMID:** 36780101
**PMCID:** PMC10356879
**DOI:** 10.1007/s40279-023-01818-z
**Access:** Free PMC
**Type:** Current Opinion + Case Series

**Key findings:**
- Case series of **5 navicular bone stress injuries** in highly competitive runners using carbon fiber plate footwear
- Mechanism: carbon plate alters foot and ankle biomechanics → increased load on navicular (midfoot bone)
- **Risk is real but rare:** primarily case-series evidence (no large RCT)
- **Recommendations:**
  1. Slow, gradual transition from habitual to carbon plate footwear (weeks, not days)
  2. Not suitable as everyday training shoe — reserve for race day or quality sessions
  3. Athletes with history of navicular or midfoot stress reactions should be cautious
  4. Sports medicine providers should ask about carbon plate use when diagnosing midfoot injuries

## Paper 7 — Beck et al. 2020 — CARBON PLATE MECHANISM QUESTIONED

**Title:** Adding carbon fiber to shoe soles may not improve running economy: a muscle-level explanation
**Authors:** Beck ON, Golyski PR, Sawicki GS
**Journal:** Scientific Reports
**Year:** 2020 (Oct 13)
**PMID:** 33051532
**PMCID:** PMC7555508
**DOI:** 10.1038/s41598-020-74097-7
**Access:** Free PMC
**Type:** Controlled trial with ultrasound (15 athletes, 4 stiffness conditions)

**Key findings:**
- Increased footwear bending stiffness did NOT affect aerobic power (p=0.458), soleus active muscle volume, or any leg muscle activity
- Did slightly lengthen ground contact time
- Hypothesis that carbon plate reduces metabolic cost by altering soleus dynamics was **not confirmed** in this study
- Mechanistic explanation for RE improvement remains debated — likely involves energy return from ZoomX foam interaction with plate, not just plate alone

**Implication:** The carbon plate in isolation may not drive the RE benefit; the combination of highly resilient midsole foam + curved plate geometry is what matters (Vaporfly-style design).

## Evidence Summary

| Question | Best evidence answer |
|---|---|
| Do shoes prevent running injuries? | No strong evidence any shoe type reduces injury rates (Cochrane 2022) |
| Should I prescribe shoes by foot type? | No — moderate-certainty evidence this doesn't reduce injuries |
| Do carbon plate shoes improve performance? | Yes — ~3–4% on level, ~2.8% on hills; curved plate + foam combination essential |
| Are carbon plate shoes safe? | Yes for occasional use; gradual transition required; navicular stress injury risk if overused |
| Minimalist vs. cushioned? | Similar injury rates; minimalist strengthens Achilles/foot but increases forefoot load |
| Best midsole for impact reduction? | Softer midsole reduces impact forces; thicker = more cushion but less ground feel |

---

## Evidence Gaps

1. Long-term injury data for carbon plate shoes (only case series currently)
2. Female-specific shoe research: underrepresented in all major RCTs
3. Optimal heel-toe drop — no consensus from RCT data
4. Trail running / off-road shoe science: almost no RCT evidence
