## Paper 1 — Pires Neves et al. 2022 ⭐ META-ANALYSIS — ORTHOSES REDUCE INJURY RISK

**Title:** Effects of Foot Orthoses on Pain and the Prevention of Lower Limb Injuries in Runners: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis
**Authors:** Pires Neves M et al.
**Journal:** Journal of Sport Rehabilitation
**Year:** 2022 (Nov 1); Epub Jul 2022
**PMID:** 35894921
**DOI:** 10.1123/jsr.2021-0302
**Type:** Systematic Review + Meta-Analysis (12 RCTs, **n=5,321 runners**)

**Key findings:**
- Control group injury rate: **37%**; foot orthoses group: **24%**
- **Risk ratio = 0.60 (95% CI 0.5–0.7, p<0.00001)** — moderate-quality evidence
- **40% reduction in lower limb injury risk** with foot orthoses vs. no orthoses
- Also significant reduction in lower limb pain

**Critical context:** This finding appears to contradict the Cochrane shoe-type review (Relph 2022). The key distinction:
- Relph 2022 compared *shoe types* (neutral vs. motion-control vs. minimalist)
- This meta-analysis evaluated *foot orthoses as an added intervention* in runners
- Orthoses modify foot mechanics more precisely than gross shoe category selection

## Paper 2 — Jor et al. 2024 ⭐ META-ANALYSIS — FO BIOMECHANICS + ECONOMY

**Title:** Effects of foot orthoses on running kinetics and kinematics: A systematic review and meta-analysis
**Authors:** Jor A, Lau NWK, Daryabor A, Koh MWP, Lam WK, Hobara H, Kobayashi T
**Journal:** Gait & Posture
**Year:** 2024 (Mar)
**PMID:** 38367456
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.gaitpost.2024.02.003
**Type:** Systematic Review + Meta-Analysis (35 studies, healthy adults)

**Key findings:**
- Both custom and off-the-shelf arch-support FOs: **↓ peak plantar pressure** at medial heel (SMD −0.35 custom, −1.03 OTS), lateral heel, and medial forefoot; **↑ pressure at mid-foot** (arch contact increases)
- Custom FOs: ↑ perceived comfort (SMD +0.36)
- Raised heel cup + arch support: **↓ initial ankle inversion** (SMD −0.58) — relevant for overpronators
- Medial post + arch support: ↓ ankle ROM (SMD −1.66) and tibial ROM (SMD −0.63) — stabilising effect
- **⚠️ Custom FOs unfavorably affected running economy** (SMD −0.25) and ↑ perceived exertion (SMD +0.20)
- Off-the-shelf FOs did not significantly worsen running economy in this analysis

**Key nuance:** Custom orthoses improve comfort and reduce pressure/inversion, but at the cost of slightly worse running economy. Off-the-shelf insoles may offer injury protection without the economy penalty.

## Paper 3 — Menéndez et al. 2020 — MTSS + ORTHOSES

**Title:** Medial Tibial Stress Syndrome in Novice and Recreational Runners: A Systematic Review
**Authors:** Menéndez C, Batalla L, Prieto A, Rodríguez MÁ, Crespo I, Olmedillas H
**Journal:** International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health
**Year:** 2020 (Oct 13)
**PMID:** 33066291
**PMCID:** PMC7602098
**DOI:** 10.3390/ijerph17207457
**Access:** Free PMC
**Type:** Systematic Review (11 studies)

**Key findings:**
- MTSS risk factors: navicular drop, foot pronation, peak hip internal rotation, high pelvic tilt
- Arch-support orthoses: ↑ contact time, normalise plantar pressure distribution, reduce pain
- Similar pain relief to shockwave therapy for MTSS
- Methodological quality of included studies is poor; results preliminary

## Paper 4 — Alexander et al. 2022 — KNEE INJURIES + ORTHOSES

**Title:** Strategies to prevent and manage running-related knee injuries: a systematic review of RCTs
**Authors:** Alexander JLN, Culvenor AG, Johnston RRT, Ezzat AM, Barton CJ
**Journal:** British Journal of Sports Medicine
**Year:** 2022 (Nov)
**PMID:** 36150753
**DOI:** 10.1136/bjsports-2022-105553
**Type:** Systematic Review + Meta-Analysis (30 RCTs)

**Key findings:**
- **Running technique retraining** (land softer): reduces knee injury risk by two-thirds (RR 0.32) — strongest finding
- **Medial-wedged foot orthoses**: reduce patellofemoral pain (PFP) in the short term (low-certainty evidence)
- Footwear options alone, exercise therapy, and graduated programs did not significantly influence knee injury risk
- Combined approach (technique + orthoses) most evidence-supported for PFP

## Paper 5 — Tang et al. 2023 — ANKLE INSTABILITY + INSOLES

**Title:** Effects of Ankle Orthoses, Taping, and Insoles on Postural Stability of Individuals with Chronic Ankle Instability
**Authors:** Tang Y et al.
**Journal:** Healthcare (Basel)
**Year:** 2023 (Sep 18)
**PMID:** 37761767
**PMCID:** PMC10530830
**Access:** Free PMC
**Type:** Systematic Review (18 studies)

**Key findings:**
- **Textured insoles**: improved postural stability via increased tactile stimulation and proprioceptive awareness — consistently positive
- **Arch-support insoles**: inconsistent results for ankle instability
- Kinesiotaping: positive effects via cutaneous receptor stimulation
- Semi-rigid orthoses: inconsistent effects on balance

**Practical:** Textured insoles are a low-cost, passive strategy to improve proprioception and balance — relevant for athletes with history of ankle sprains (e.g., unihockey).

## Paper 6 — Tourillon et al. 2019 ⭐ FOOT STRENGTH FRAMEWORK

**Title:** How to Evaluate and Improve Foot Strength in Athletes: An Update
**Authors:** Tourillon R, Gojanovic B, Fourchet F
**Journal:** Frontiers in Sports and Active Living
**Year:** 2019 (Oct 11)
**PMID:** 33344969
**PMCID:** PMC7739583
**Access:** Free PMC
**Type:** Review (Swiss Olympic Medical Center)

**Physiology framework:**
- Intrinsic foot muscles (IFM) are the primary local foot stabilisers
- During running: IFM lengthen eccentrically in stance, shorten at propulsion (arch recoil)
- IFM support medial longitudinal arch, control pronation, provide shock absorption
- **Windlass mechanism**: toe dorsiflexion tensions plantar fascia → stiffens foot for push-off
- Weak IFM → reduced arch stiffness → energy loss at push-off → ↑ injury risk

**Effective strengthening exercises:**
- **Short foot exercise** (arch doming without toe curling) — most specific
- **Toe doming / spread** — activates intrinsic without extrinsic dominance
- **Towel/marble pickups** — flexor digitorum brevis
- **Hopping drills** — dynamic; closest to running demands
- **Barefoot running** — natural progressive load

## Paper 7 — Ridge et al. 2019 ⭐ MINIMALIST WALKING = FOOT EXERCISES RCT

**Title:** Walking in Minimalist Shoes Is Effective for Strengthening Foot Muscles
**Authors:** Ridge ST, Olsen MT, Bruening DA, Jurgensmeier K, Griffin D, Davis IS, Johnson AW
**Journal:** Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise
**Year:** 2019 (Jan)
**PMID:** 30113521
**DOI:** 10.1249/MSS.0000000000001751
**Type:** RCT (57 runners; 3 groups: minimalist walking, foot exercises, control; 8 weeks)

**Key findings:**
- Both minimalist walking (MSW) and foot strengthening (FS) groups: **significant ↑ in all foot muscle size and strength** from weeks 0–8
- Control group: no changes
- Changes appeared as early as **week 4** in both active groups
- No difference between MSW and FS groups — both equally effective
- **Practical advantage of MSW**: changing footwear requires less compliance effort than dedicated exercises

**Protocol:** Walking in minimalist shoes progressively (increasing daily step count each week), maintaining regular running mileage unchanged.

## Paper 8 — Xu et al. 2023 — MINIMALIST SHOES: SYSTEMATIC REVIEW OF IFM

**Title:** The Effects of Minimalist Shoes on Plantar Intrinsic Foot Muscle Size and Strength: A Systematic Review
**Authors:** Xu J, Saliba SA, Jaffri AH
**Journal:** International Journal of Sports Medicine
**Year:** 2023 (May)
**PMID:** 35878616
**DOI:** 10.1055/a-1908-8867
**Type:** Systematic Review (9 studies, ≥2 week interventions with control)

**Key findings:**
- Significant increases in IFM **strength: 9–57%** across studies
- Significant increases in IFM **size (volume, CSA, thickness): 7–11%**
- Supports minimalist shoes as a convenient, effective IFM training tool
- Caveat: transition must be gradual to avoid injury

## Paper 9 — Taddei et al. 2020 ⭐ FOOT STRENGTHENING RCT — RUNNING MECHANICS

**Title:** Effects of a foot strengthening program on foot muscle morphology and running mechanics: A proof-of-concept RCT
**Authors:** Taddei UT, Matias AB, Ribeiro FIA, Bus SA, Sacco ICN
**Journal:** Physical Therapy in Sport
**Year:** 2020 (Mar)
**PMID:** 31962191
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.ptsp.2020.01.007
**Type:** Single-blind RCT (28 recreational runners; 8 weeks foot strengthening vs. control)

**Key findings:**
- 8-week IFM strengthening program: **↑ volume of all foot muscles** vs. control
- **↑ vertical propulsive impulse** during running (more force at push-off)
- Correlation: vertical propulsive impulse ~ volume of abductor hallucis (r=0.40), abductor digiti minimi (r=0.41), and flexor digitorum brevis (r=0.69)
- First RCT linking IFM training to measurable running mechanics improvement

**Implication:** Foot strengthening isn't just injury prevention — it can improve running efficiency and propulsive force generation. Accessible to any runner.

## Paper 10 — Arnold & Moody 2018 ⭐ CLINICAL REFERENCE — COMMON RUNNING INJURIES

**Title:** Common Running Injuries: Evaluation and Management
**Authors:** Arnold MJ, Moody AL
**Journal:** American Family Physician
**Year:** 2018 (Apr 15)
**PMID:** 29671490
**Type:** Clinical Review

**Most common running injuries (survey data):**
1. Iliotibial band syndrome (34%)
2. Plantar fasciitis / fasciopathy (30%)
3. Strained thigh/hip muscle (25%)
4. Medial tibial stress syndrome / shin splints (22%)

**Plantar fasciopathy treatment (evidence-supported):**
- **Stretching** (plantar fascia, calf/Achilles) — first-line
- **Heel raises / eccentric loading** — tendon remodelling
- **Arch-support orthoses** — significantly improve symptoms
- Rest, ice, NSAIDs — symptomatic relief only

**Key rule:** 80% of runners continue running with injuries; early intervention prevents chronic cases.

## Paper 11 — MacGabhann et al. 2022 — BAREFOOT GRASS RUNNING FOR PLANTAR FASCIITIS

**Title:** Barefoot Running on Grass as a Potential Treatment for Plantar Fasciitis
**Authors:** MacGabhann S, Kearney D, Perrem N, Francis P
**Journal:** International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health
**Year:** 2022 (Nov 22)
**PMID:** 36497540
**PMCID:** PMC9741467
**Access:** Free PMC
**Type:** Prospective Case Series (20/28 patients, 6-week programme)
**⚠️ Conflict: senior author receives honorarium from Vivo Barefoot**

**Key findings:**
- 6-week progressive barefoot running on grass: pain at 6 weeks ↓ 38.8% from baseline
- Pain at 12 weeks: ↓ 58.3%
- 19/20 patients improved
- Mechanism: natural surface reduces peak plantar pressure; IFM activation increases; gait naturally adapts toward forefoot
- Allows continuation of running during treatment (important for athletes)

**Caveat:** Case series design, conflict of interest, no control group. Promising but not definitive.

## Evidence Summary

### Orthoses / Insoles

| Question | Best evidence | Answer |
|---|---|---|
| Do insoles prevent running injuries? | Meta-analysis (Pires Neves 2022, n=5321, moderate quality) | Yes — 40% injury risk reduction |
| Do custom vs. off-the-shelf differ? | Meta-analysis (Jor 2024) | Custom = better pressure relief + comfort; BUT ↓ running economy; OTS adequate for most |
| Do insoles help MTSS (shin splints)? | Systematic review | Yes — normalise pressure, reduce pain |
| Do insoles help patellofemoral pain? | Systematic review (Alexander 2022) | Medial-wedged FOs reduce PFP short-term |
| Do insoles fix ankle instability? | Systematic review (Tang 2023) | Textured insoles improve proprioception; arch-support inconsistent |

### Foot Strengthening

| Question | Evidence | Answer |
|---|---|---|
| Does foot strengthening improve running mechanics? | RCT (Taddei 2020) | Yes — ↑ propulsive impulse; IFM volume correlates with push-off force |
| Is minimalist walking a valid alternative to exercises? | RCT (Ridge 2019) | Yes — equivalent IFM gains; better compliance |
| How much strength gain from minimalist shoes? | Systematic review (Xu 2023) | 9–57% strength, 7–11% size increase |

### Plantar Fasciitis

| Intervention | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Stretching (plantar fascia + calf) | Strong — first-line |
| Eccentric heel raises | Strong — tendon remodelling |
| Arch-support orthoses | Strong — significantly improves symptoms |
| Barefoot grass running | Preliminary — case series, promising |
| Shockwave therapy (ESWT) | Moderate — effective for chronic cases (Rhim 2024) |

---

## Evidence Gaps

1. Long-term RCTs on custom vs. off-the-shelf orthoses for recreational runners
2. Optimal IFM training dose and frequency not established
3. Whether pre-emptive foot strengthening (before symptoms) prevents injury — RCT data lacking
4. Plantar fasciitis: no large RCT comparing all treatments head-to-head
5. Unihockey-specific foot loading data: not available; extrapolated from multidirectional sport literature
