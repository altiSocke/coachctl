Synthesised into: wiki/general/gear.md --- ## SECTION A — FOAM ROLLING (SELF-MYOFASCIAL RELEASE) --- ## Paper 1 — Martínez-Aranda et al. 2024 ⭐ PRIMARY SYSTEMATIC REVIEW Title: Effects of Self-Myofascial Release on Athletes' Physical Performance: A Systematic Review Authors: Martínez-Aranda LM, Sanz-Matesanz M, García-Mantilla ED, González-Fernández FT Journal: Journal of Functional Morphology and Kinesiology Year: 2024 (Jan 11) PMID: 38249097 PMCID: PMC10801590 DOI: 10.3390/jfmk9010020 Access: Free PMC Type: Systematic Review (25 studies, n=517 athletes, databases through Sep 2023) Key findings: - Acute positive effects on flexibility and range of motion (ROM): consistent finding across studies — SMR improves ROM acutely - No negative effect on maximal strength and power: SMR pre-training/warm-up does NOT impair force production (unlike prolonged static stretching) - Positive effects on perceptual recovery and DOMS reduction: athletes subjectively feel better and perceive less soreness - Some evidence of positive effects on agility and very short high-speed actions (sprinting) - Chronic effects on performance: heterogeneous results — no strong consensus for long-term performance gains - Evidence heterogeneity: protocol parameters (duration, pressure, speed) vary greatly across studies Practical takeaway: Use foam rolling before sessions to improve ROM without strength penalties; use after sessions to reduce DOMS and improve recovery perception.
