Synthesised into: wiki/general/stretching.md , wiki/general/running.md , wiki/general/cycling.md --- ## Paper 1 — Warneke et al. 2025 ⭐ PRIMARY META-ANALYSIS Title: Acute and Chronic Effects of Stretching on Running Economy: A Systematic Review with Meta-Analysis Authors: Warneke K, Zechner M, Siegel SD, Jochum D, Brunssen L, Konrad A Journal: Sports Medicine Open Year: 2025 (published May 30) PMID: 40442558 PMCID: PMC12122984 DOI: 10.1186/s40798-025-00859-0 Access: Free PMC full text Type: Systematic Review + Meta-Analysis (PRISMA 2020, PICOS, robust variance estimation) Quality: PEDro score 4.88 (fair); GRADE: low certainty of evidence Key findings: - 15 acute studies + 2 chronic studies, n=181 total participants - No significant effect of any stretching type (SS, DS, PNF) on running economy acutely (p=0.21–0.65) - The old belief that stretching reduces tendon stiffness → impairs RE is NOT supported at normal warm-up durations - Prolonged/extreme stretching (>60s per muscle group) remains theoretically problematic for stiffness-dependent RE — not tested at typical warm-up durations - Chronic effects: only 2 studies found — insufficient for meta-analysis; large evidence gap - Conclusion: it is unreasonable to avoid pre-running stretching to prevent RE decreases Methodological limits: small sample sizes; heterogeneous protocols; no mechanistic measurement of stiffness; RE tested at varying velocities (65–90% VO2max).
