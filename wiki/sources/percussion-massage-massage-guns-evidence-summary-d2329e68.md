## Summary

Percussion Massage (Massage Guns) — Evidence Summary Last updated: 2026-04-28 | Sources: peer-reviewed literature 2019–2024, NSCA/ACSM consensus --- ## What is it? Percussive therapy devices (commonly "massage guns" — Theragun, Hypervolt, Ekrin, etc.) deliver rapid, repetitive axial pressure strokes to soft tissue at: - Frequency: 20–53 Hz (1200–3180 RPM typical) - Amplitude: 10–16 mm stroke depth (varies by device/head) - Duration per site: typically 30–120 s in practice; studies use 30–300 s The mechanism combines vibration therapy and myofascial compression , activating mechanoreceptors (Golgi tendon organs, Ruffini endings) and suppressing nociception via gate control. --- ## What the evidence shows ### ✅ Well-supported effects | Effect | Evidence level | Notes | |---|---|---| | Acute ROM increase (5–15°) | Strong (multiple RCTs) | Comparable to static stretching without flexibility loss | | Delayed-onset muscle soreness (DOMS) reduction | Moderate | Best when applied immediately post-exercise and at 24 h | | Perceived recovery / RPE improvement | Moderate | Likely neurological / placebo-assisted; measurable nonetheless | | Short-term pain reduction | Moderate | Gate-control mechanism; analgesic effect lasts 30–60 min | | Pre-activation warm-up effect | Moderate | 30–60 s per muscle group pre-exercise → improved force production | ### ❌ Not well-supported | Claim | Reality | |---|---| | Accelerates metabolic waste clearance (lactate) | No RCT evidence; lactate clears via aerobic metabolism regardless | | Replaces warm-up | ROM gains are acute; still need CV warm-up for hard sessions | | Structural tissue remodeling | No evidence for chronic tissue change from percussive therapy alone | | Superior to foam rolling for recovery | Comparable outcomes; no consistent superiority | --- ## Mechanisms 1.
