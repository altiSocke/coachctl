# Greifenseelauf — Race Facts

**Full name:** Greifenseelauf, Uster (also: Halbmarathon Greifensee)
**Organiser:** Markus Ryffel's AG, Worb
**Website:** greifenseelauf.ch | **Registration:** trackmaxx.ch (code GSL26)
**Schedule:** Always third Saturday of September.

## Course

- Loops the Greifensee nature reserve near Uster, Canton Zurich.
- **Surface:** ~60% asphalt, ~40% natural roads / gravel.
- **Start:** Seestrasse, Uster (~10 min walk from village/finish area).
- **Finish:** Stadtpark Uster.
- **Timing:** Net chip timing (clock starts at your start-line crossing).
  - Exception: Top 10 Overall timed by gun time.
- **Seeding:** By best time from last 3 years; first-timers self-declare.

## Pacemakers

Available for HM finish times: **1:30, 1:45, 2:00, 2:15** (and intermediate targets).
Pacers carry a flag rucksack with target time printed.

## Logistics

- **Startnumber pickup:** Stadthalle Uster (or postal delivery option).
- **Bag drop / valuables:** Stadthalle (envelope + coupon system).
- **Changing rooms + showers:** Berufsbildungszentrum Uster (on route to start).
- **Medical:** 11 medical posts + ambulance + doctor on course.
- **Public transport:** SBB SwissRunners ticket (code on registration confirmation).

## Racing notes

- Mixed surface (60/40 asphalt/gravel) means trail shoes or versatile trainers preferred over pure road shoes.
- Net timing rewards a calm start — no need to sprint off the line.
- Aid stations are well spaced: largest gap is 7.4 → 11.5 km (4.1 km). Gel at km ~5 covers you to the banana station at 11.5 km.
- Pacemaker for 1:30 is available — useful if targeting sub-1:30.
- The km 17.8–19.5 aid station (Talacker) is the last chance to take water before the finish; don't skip it.
