- --- ## The Foot as an Active Structure The foot is not a passive platform — it is an active, spring-like system that directly affects running efficiency and injury risk. [source:foot-health-science-reference-03307eb2]
- Key structures: - Medial longitudinal arch (MLA): acts as a spring — stores and returns elastic energy during stance - Plantar fascia: the passive tensile element; stiffens the arch via the windlass mechanism (toe extension → tension → arch locks) - Intrinsic foot muscles (IFM): the active element — eccentrically control arch drop in early stance, concentrically stiffen the foot at push-off - Extrinsic muscles (tibialis posterior, peroneals, FHL, FDL): control ankle and rearfoot, transmit force from calf complex Consequence for runners: weak IFM → excessive arch deformation under load → energy leak at push-off → ↑ plantar fascia stress → ↑ navicular/metatarsal load → injury chain.
