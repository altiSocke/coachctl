- 2020 ⭐ PRIMARY BIOMECHANICS REVIEW Title: Systematic Review of the Role of Footwear Constructions in Running Biomechanics: Implications for Running-Related Injury and Performance Authors: Sun X, Lam WK, Zhang X, Wang J, Fu W Journal: Journal of Sports Science and Medicine Year: 2020 (Feb 24); eCollection 2020 Mar PMID: 32132824 PMCID: PMC7039038 Access: Free PMC full text Type: Systematic Review (63 studies, databases through Sep 2018) Key findings — shoe constructions: - Stiffness: Increasing midsole bending stiffness at an optimal range improves performance-related variables (energy return); too little = poor energy return, too much = restricted foot motion - Cushioning (midsole softness): Softer midsoles reduce impact forces and loading rates; thicker midsoles provide better shock attenuation but may decrease plantar sensory feedback - Minimalist shoes: Improve running economy and increase Achilles tendon cross-sectional area and stiffness; but increase metatarsophalangeal and ankle joint loading compared to conventional shoes - Heel-toe drop: Evidence insufficient to draw clear conclusions on injury risk; further research needed - Heel cup, upper, shoe lace, heel flare, MBT (rocker sole): Insufficient evidence for guidelines Practical summary: - No single shoe construction eliminates injury risk across all runners - Stiffer midsole (e.g., carbon plate) → better RE, longer contact time, greater stride length - Softer midsole → lower impact forces, but increased energy cost --- ## Paper 2 — Relph et al.
